

# Coherent Diffractive Imaging Phase Retrieval for Copper Pad Topography

## [Problem Formulation]

### Forward Model

Given a complex-valued 2D object reflection function $O(\mathbf{r}) \in \mathbb{C}^{N \times N}$ defined on a discrete grid with pixel size $\Delta x = 27\,\text{nm}$, the measured far-field diffraction intensity is:

$$I(\mathbf{k}) = \left|\mathcal{F}\left\{P(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{R}_j) \cdot O(\mathbf{r})\right\}\right|^2 + n$$

where:
- $\mathcal{F}$ is the 2D Discrete Fourier Transform (Fraunhofer far-field propagation)
- $P(\mathbf{r})$ is the complex-valued illumination probe function (Airy spot or structured speckle)
- $\mathbf{R}_j$ is the $j$-th scan position in ptychographic mode
- $n \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)$ is additive Gaussian noise (parameter unspecified in the paper)
- $I(\mathbf{k}) \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}^{2048 \times 2048}$ is the measured intensity on the CCD detector

### Object Model

The object $O(\mathbf{r})$ is a reflective sample consisting of a $15 \times 15$ periodic array of Cu pads embedded in a planar substrate stack. The complex reflection function is:

$$O(\mathbf{r}) = \begin{cases} R_{\text{Cu}} \cdot \exp\!\left(i\,\frac{4\pi\, h(\mathbf{r})}{\lambda}\right), & \mathbf{r} \in S_{\text{pad}} \\ R_p, & \mathbf{r} \notin S_{\text{pad}} \end{cases}$$

where:
- $R_{\text{Cu}}$ is the complex reflectivity of Cu at $\lambda = 13.5\,\text{nm}$ (from CXRO/Henke tables, computed via transfer matrix method through the multilayer: vacuum → SiCN → SiO₂ → Si substrate, with Cu replacing the stack within pad regions)
- $R_p$ is the known complex reflectivity of the surrounding planar layer (substrate stack without Cu)
- $h(\mathbf{r}) \in [1, 3]\,\text{nm}$ is the recession depth of each Cu pad (randomly assigned per pad)
- $\lambda = 13.5\,\text{nm}$, yielding phase modulation $\Delta\Phi = \frac{4\pi h}{\lambda} \in [0.93, 2.79]\,\text{rad}$ (no phase wrapping)

### Variable Definitions

| Symbol | Definition | Value/Domain |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| $\lambda$ | EUV wavelength | 13.5 nm |
| $\Delta x$ | Pixel size | 27 nm |
| $N_{\text{pad}}$ | Pixels per pad | 22 |
| CD | Critical dimension | $\sim$600 nm ($\approx 22 \times 27$) |
| Pitch | Pad pitch | $\sim$800 nm ($\approx 30 \times 27$) |
| Duty cycle | CD/Pitch | 0.6 |
| Array | Pad array size | $15 \times 15$ |
| $h$ | Pad recession depth | $[1, 3]$ nm |
| $\beta$ | Feedback parameter | $(0, 1]$ (tunable) |
| $\alpha$ | PIE regularization | Small positive constant |
| Step size | Ptychographic scan step | 40 pixels ($\sim$1080 nm) |
| Overlap | Probe overlap | $\sim$75% |

### Inverse Problem

Given only $I(\mathbf{k})$ (intensity = squared modulus of the Fourier transform of the exit wave), recover $O(\mathbf{r})$. This is the **phase retrieval problem**: we have lost the Fourier phase $\phi(\mathbf{k}) = \arg\!\left[\mathcal{F}\{P \cdot O\}\right]$ and must recover it using:

1. **Fourier-domain constraint**: The magnitude $|\mathcal{F}\{\cdot\}|$ must equal $\sqrt{I(\mathbf{k})}$
2. **Real-space constraints**: Support $S$, known substrate reflectivity $R_p$, and physical bounds on $|O|$ and $\arg(O)$

## [Proposed Strategy]

### Two Iterative Projection Algorithms

**1. rpIO (Reflective-Prior Input–Output)** — for single-shot CDI (no scanning):
- A modified Hybrid Input–Output (HIO) algorithm that replaces the standard support-only constraint with a **reflectivity prior** on the substrate region.
- Alternates between Fourier modulus replacement and real-space prior enforcement.

**2. rpPIE (Reflective-Prior Ptychographic Iterative Engine)** — for ptychographic CDI:
- A modified ePIE algorithm augmented with the same reflectivity prior.
- Exploits redundancy from overlapping probe positions for robust convergence.
- Two illumination modes: (a) Airy spot, (b) structured speckle field.

### Rationale for Strategy Selection

- **Not a learning problem**: No training data, no neural networks. This is a classical iterative projection / alternating minimization problem.
- **Stability**: HIO/PIE are the gold-standard algorithms for phase retrieval, well-understood convergence properties.
- **The key innovation** is the reflectivity prior $R_p$ — a physics-informed constraint that dramatically improves convergence (2 orders of magnitude error reduction).
- **ADMM/Primal-Dual** could be used but would be over-engineering; the modulus replacement + prior projection scheme is the natural and paper-specified approach.

## [Step-by-Step Plan]

### Step 1: Data Preprocessing — Material Optical Constants & Object Construction

**1.1 Retrieve optical constants from CXRO/Henke database at $\lambda = 13.5\,\text{nm}$:**

| Material | $n$ (refractive index) | $k$ (extinction) |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Cu | $n_{\text{Cu}}, k_{\text{Cu}}$ | from Henke tables |
| SiCN | $n_{\text{SiCN}}, k_{\text{SiCN}}$ | from Henke tables |
| SiO₂ | $n_{\text{SiO2}}, k_{\text{SiO2}}$ | from Henke tables |
| Si | $n_{\text{Si}}, k_{\text{Si}}$ | from Henke tables |

At EUV, the complex refractive index is $\tilde{n} = 1 - \delta + i\beta$, with $\delta$ and $\beta$ tabulated. Reflectivity is computed via the **transfer matrix method (TMM)** for the multilayer stack.

**1.2 Compute complex reflectivities:**
- $R_p$: Reflectivity of the planar substrate stack (SiCN/SiO₂/Si) — this is the **known prior**.
- $R_{\text{Cu}}$: Reflectivity of Cu pad regions (Cu replacing part of the stack).
- Use Fresnel coefficients and TMM for each interface. The layer thicknesses must be assumed or inferred from typical hybrid bonding geometries (the paper references specific layer structures).

**1.3 Construct the ground-truth object $O_{\text{true}}(\mathbf{r})$:**

```
Grid size: N_obj × N_obj where N_obj accommodates 15 pads × pitch_pixels
  pitch_pixels = round(800 nm / 27 nm) ≈ 30 pixels
  pad_pixels = 22 pixels (given)
  N_obj = 15 × 30 = 450 pixels (object region)

For each pad (i, j) where i, j ∈ {0, ..., 14}:
  - Center position: (i × 30 + 15, j × 30 + 15)
  - Pad region: square of 22 × 22 pixels centered at pad center
  - Assign random recession depth: h_{i,j} ~ Uniform(1, 3) nm
  - Phase: ΔΦ_{i,j} = 4π h_{i,j} / 13.5
  - O(r) = R_Cu · exp(i · ΔΦ_{i,j}) within pad
  - O(r) = R_p outside all pads
```

**1.4 Define the support mask:**
$$S = \bigcup_{i,j} S_{\text{pad}}^{(i,j)}$$
where $S_{\text{pad}}^{(i,j)}$ is the $22 \times 22$ pixel region of pad $(i,j)$.

**1.5 Zero-pad the object** to the detector size $2048 \times 2048$ (or to a size satisfying the oversampling requirement $\sigma = N_{\text{det}} / N_{\text{obj}} > 2$ in each dimension; here $2048/450 \approx 4.6$, well oversampled).

### Step 2: Forward Operator Implementation (Physics)

**2.1 Probe function construction:**

**(a) Airy spot probe:**
$$P_{\text{Airy}}(\mathbf{r}) \propto \left[\frac{2\,J_1(\pi r / r_0)}{\pi r / r_0}\right]^2$$
where $r_0$ is determined by the numerical aperture. The probe should illuminate a region large enough to cover several pads but small enough for ptychographic diversity. Exact $r_0$ must be chosen to match the paper's geometry (probe diameter should be such that 40-pixel step gives 75% overlap, implying probe diameter $\approx 160$ pixels, i.e., $\sim$4.3 µm).

**(b) Structured speckle probe:**
$$P_{\text{speckle}}(\mathbf{r}) = \mathcal{F}^{-1}\left\{\text{circ}(|\mathbf{k}|/k_{\max}) \cdot \exp(i\,\phi_{\text{rand}}(\mathbf{k}))\right\}$$
where $\phi_{\text{rand}}$ is a random phase screen, producing a speckle-like illumination with the same spatial extent.

**2.2 Exit wave formation:**
$$\Psi(\mathbf{r}, \mathbf{R}_j) = P(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{R}_j) \cdot O(\mathbf{r})$$

**2.3 Far-field propagation (Fraunhofer):**
$$\hat{\Psi}(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{R}_j) = \mathcal{F}\{\Psi(\mathbf{r}, \mathbf{R}_j)\}$$

Implemented as `numpy.fft.fft2` with `fftshift` for centering. The Fraunhofer condition $z \gg 2d^2/\lambda$ is assumed satisfied.

**2.4 Intensity measurement:**
$$I_j(\mathbf{k}) = |\hat{\Psi}(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{R}_j)|^2 + n_j$$

where $n_j \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_n^2)$. Since the paper does not specify $\sigma_n$, we parameterize it and test with SNR values of $\{20, 30, 40\}\,\text{dB}$ (or equivalently, set $\sigma_n$ as a fraction of $\max(I)$, e.g., 1%, 0.1%).

**2.5 Ptychographic scan positions:**
Generate scan positions $\{\mathbf{R}_j\}$ on a regular grid with step size 40 pixels in both $x$ and $y$, covering the full object field. Number of positions:
$$N_{\text{scan}} = \left\lfloor\frac{N_{\text{obj}} - N_{\text{probe}}}{\text{step}}\right\rfloor + 1 \quad \text{in each dimension}$$
For single-shot rpIO: $\mathbf{R} = \mathbf{0}$ (single illumination, probe covers entire object or is a plane wave).

### Step 3: Solver / Algorithm Architecture Details

#### Algorithm A: rpIO (Reflective-Prior Input–Output)

```
Initialize: O_0(r) = random complex (or R_p everywhere with random phase on S)
             Set β ∈ (0, 1) (feedback parameter, typically β = 0.9)

For n = 0, 1, 2, ..., N_iter:
    
    # Step 3a: Forward propagate
    Ψ_n(k) = FFT2(O_n(r))    [zero-padded to 2048×2048]
    
    # Step 3b: Fourier modulus replacement
    Ψ_c(k) = √I(k) · Ψ_n(k) / |Ψ_n(k)|
    # Handle zeros: where |Ψ_n(k)| < ε, set Ψ_c(k) = 0
    
    # Step 3c: Inverse propagate
    O'(r) = IFFT2(Ψ_c(k))    [crop to object region]
    
    # Step 3d: Real-space constraint with reflectivity prior
    For r ∈ S (pad support):
        O_{n+1}(r) = O'(r)    # Accept Fourier-constrained estimate
    For r ∉ S (substrate):
        O_{n+1}(r) = β · O_n(r) + (1 - β) · R_p    # Enforce prior
    
    # Step 3e: Compute error metric
    E_n = Σ_k | |FFT2(O_{n+1})| - √I(k) |² / Σ_k I(k)
```

**Key difference from standard HIO**: Outside the support, instead of $O_{n+1} = O_n - \beta \cdot O'$ (HIO feedback), we use the **reflectivity prior** $R_p$ as the attractor, blending with the current estimate.

#### Algorithm B: rpPIE (Reflective-Prior Ptychographic Iterative Engine)

```
Initialize: O_0(r) = R_p everywhere (uniform substrate reflectivity)
            P(r) = known probe function (Airy or speckle)
            Set β ∈ (0, 1), α > 0 (small regularizer)

For n = 0, 1, 2, ..., N_iter:
  For each scan position R_j (randomize order each epoch):
    
    # Step 3a: Form exit wave
    Ψ_n(r, R_j) = P(r - R_j) · O_n(r)
    
    # Step 3b: Forward propagate
    Ψ̂_n(k) = FFT2(Ψ_n(r, R_j))
    
    # Step 3c: Fourier modulus replacement
    Ψ̂_c(k) = √I_j(k) · Ψ̂_n(k) / |Ψ̂_n(k)|
    
    # Step 3d: Back-propagate
    Ψ_c(r, R_j) = IFFT2(Ψ̂_c(k))
    
    # Step 3e: Object update (ePIE-style)
    ΔΨ(r) = Ψ_c(r, R_j) - Ψ_n(r, R_j)
    
    O_{n+1}(r) = O_n(r) + [|P(r-R_j)| / |P_max|] · 
                 [P*(r-R_j) / (|P(r-R_j)|² + α)] · β · ΔΨ(r)
    
    # Step 3f: Reflectivity prior enforcement
    For r ∉ S:
        O_{n+1}(r) = β · O_{n+1}(r) + (1 - β) · R_p
    
  # Step 3g: Compute reconstruction error after full scan cycle
  E_n = || O_{n+1} - O_true ||² / || O_true ||²
```

**Key implementation notes:**
- $|P_{\max}| = \max_{\mathbf{r}} |P(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{R}_j)|$ — maximum probe amplitude for normalization
- $\alpha \sim 10^{-3} \cdot |P_{\max}|^2$ — regularization to avoid division by zero
- The prior enforcement in Step 3f is applied **after** the ePIE update, only outside the support
- Scan positions should be traversed in **random order** each iteration to avoid systematic bias

### Step 4: Loss Function, Error Metrics & Evaluation Protocol

**4.1 Fourier-domain error (convergence monitor):**
$$E_{\text{Fourier}}^{(n)} = \frac{\sum_{\mathbf{k}} \left| |\hat{\Psi}_n(\mathbf{k})| - \sqrt{I(\mathbf{k})} \right|^2}{\sum_{\mathbf{k}} I(\mathbf{k})}$$

**4.2 Object-domain reconstruction error (ground-truth comparison):**
$$E_{\text{obj}}^{(n)} = \frac{\| O_n - O_{\text{true}} \|_2^2}{\| O_{\text{true}} \|_2^2}$$

Note: Before computing $E_{\text{obj}}$, resolve the global phase ambiguity by aligning $O_n$ to $O_{\text{true}}$ via:
$$\theta^* = \arg\min_\theta \| O_n \cdot e^{i\theta} - O_{\text{true}} \|^2 \implies \theta^* = \arg\left(\sum_{\mathbf{r}} O_n^*(\mathbf{r}) \cdot O_{\text{true}}(\mathbf{r})\right)$$

**4.3 Phase error metric (metrology-relevant):**
$$\bar{\epsilon}_\phi = \frac{1}{|S|} \sum_{\mathbf{r} \in S} \left| \arg(O_n(\mathbf{r})) - \arg(O_{\text{true}}(\mathbf{r})) \right|$$

Target values from the paper:
- Airy-spot rpPIE: $\bar{\epsilon}_\phi \approx 2.6 \times 10^{-2}\,\text{rad}$
- Speckle-field rpPIE: $\bar{\epsilon}_\phi \approx 4.1 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{rad}$

**4.4 Topography error (derived):**
$$\bar{\epsilon}_h = \frac{\lambda}{4\pi} \cdot \bar{\epsilon}_\phi$$
- For $\bar{\epsilon}_\phi = 4.1 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{rad}$: $\bar{\epsilon}_h \approx 0.0009\,\text{nm}$ (sub-picometer precision)

**4.5 Convergence criteria:**
- Run for $N_{\text{iter}} = 200\text{–}500$ iterations (rpIO) or epochs (rpPIE)
- Monitor $E_{\text{obj}}^{(n)}$ on log scale; expect 2 orders of magnitude improvement over standard HIO/PIE
- Convergence: $|E^{(n+1)} - E^{(n)}| / E^{(n)} < 10^{-6}$

### Step 5: Baseline Comparisons

**5.1 Standard HIO (baseline — expected to fail/not converge):**
Same as rpIO but replace Step 3d with:
$$O_{n+1}(\mathbf{r}) = \begin{cases} O'(\mathbf{r}), & \mathbf{r} \in S \\ O_n(\mathbf{r}) - \beta \cdot O'(\mathbf{r}), & \mathbf{r} \notin S \end{cases}$$

**5.2 Standard PIE (baseline — converges but with higher error):**
Same as rpPIE but **omit Step 3f** (no reflectivity prior enforcement).

### Step 6: Implementation & Numerical Considerations

**6.1 FFT conventions:**
- Use `numpy.fft.fft2` / `numpy.fft.ifft2` with proper normalization (`norm='ortho'` or manual $1/N$)
- Apply `fftshift` / `ifftshift` consistently for centered diffraction patterns
- Ensure the zero-frequency component is at the center of the detector

**6.2 Oversampling & zero-padding:**
- Object array: $N_{\text{obj}} \approx 450 \times 450$ pixels
- Embed in $2048 \times 2048$ array (zero-padded) for FFT → oversampling ratio $\sigma \approx 4.6 > 2$ ✓
- For ptychography: the exit wave $\Psi$ has extent equal to the probe diameter; zero-pad each exit wave to $2048 \times 2048$ before FFT

**6.3 Probe normalization:**
- Normalize probe such that $\sum |P(\mathbf{r})|^2 = 1$ (or to total photon count)

**6.4 Random seed control:**
- Fix random seed for recession depths $h_{i,j}$, noise realizations, and initial guesses to ensure reproducibility

**6.5 Data types:**
- All complex arrays: `numpy.complex128` (double precision) to preserve phase accuracy at the $10^{-4}$ rad level
- Intensity arrays: `numpy.float64`
